What we're raising advocacy for:

Flooding remains a major threat to many communities, driven by factors such as heavy rainfall, inadequate drainage systems, deforestation, and improper waste disposal. These causes combine to overwhelm waterways and push floodwaters into homes and streets. Addressing these root issues through environmental protection and infrastructure improvement is essential to reducing long-term risks.

Communities, schools, and local governments must collaborate to strengthen preparedness and resilience. By investing in early-warning systems, proper urban planning, and public education on flood risks, we help people respond effectively to these recurring hazards. A unified commitment to mitigation and preparedness safeguards vulnerable populations and ensures a safer, more resilient future for all.

Article 1

Strengthening Flood Control Infrastructure in Butuan City

Butuan City can lessen flooding by improving structural defenses such as river dikes, floodwalls, and drainage systems, along with adding pumping stations in low-lying areas. Regular de-silting of waterways and maintenance of drainage canals ensure that water flows properly during heavy rainfall.

"Ang pagpatuman sa Urban Drainage Project sa dakbayan sa Butuan kay aron makabaton ang syudad sa limpyo ug hapsay nga kadalanan ug dili na masinati ang pagbaha sa kadalanan labi na kung sunod-sunod ang pagbundak sa kusog nga ulan."

Nature-based solutions can further support these engineering measures. Retention basins, rainwater harvesting, expanded green spaces, and restored riparian buffers help slow runoff and prevent river swelling. Coordinated efforts from local authorities and communities make these strategies more effective.

- Mayor Ronnie Vicente C. Lagnada

Article 2

Environmental and Community-Based Strategies

Protecting the Agusan Marsh, watersheds, and upstream forests reduces runoff and enhances natural water absorption. Regulating logging, mining, and land conversion helps preserve forest cover and maintain ecological stability.

Community participation also strengthens flood resilience. Early warning systems, disaster training, evacuation planning, and household flood-proofing help residents prepare for flood events. These efforts, supported by good governance and regular monitoring, improve Butuan City's ability to adapt to recurring floods.
